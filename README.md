# Sales Performance & Profitability Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes sales performance and profitability for a multi-country retail company using Power BI.  
The goal is to identify key trends, performance drivers, and actionable business insights to support data-driven decision-making.

# Dashboard Structure
## Page 1 - Executive Overview
Designed for decision-makers, this page summarizes high-level performance:
- Total Sales & Total Profit
- Profit Margin
- Number of Orders & Average Order Value
- Year-over-Year Sales & Profit growth
- Sales & Profit Trends over time
- Profit Distribution by Category
This page enables executives to quickly assess financial performance and value concentration.

## Page 2 - Product & Segment Deep Dive
This page focuses on analytical exploration and performance drivers:
- Sales & Profit Scatter plot (Product-level performance) 
- Margin Analysis by Category  
- Sales & Profit by Customer Segment 
- Top 10 products by Total Profit  
It helps identify high-performing products, inefficient revenue generators, and segment-level profitability dynamics.x

## Key KPIs
- Total Sales  
- Total Profit  
- Profit Margin (%)  
- Number of Orders  
- Average Order Value  
- Sales Growth (YoY)  

## Key Insights
- Technology is the primary profit driver, contributing the highest share of total profit across categories.
- The Consumer segment generates the highest sales volume, acting as the main revenue engine of the business.
- While overall Year-over-Year growth is positive, profitability is not evenly distributed across product lines.
- The Tables sub-category (Furniture) shows negative profitability, indicating structural margin issues despite ongoing sales activity.
- Additionally, at least one high-revenue product presents a relatively low profit margin (13.8%), suggesting potential pricing or discount inefficiencies

## Main Takeaway
The most critical finding was that despite overall positive growth, profitability is highly concentrated in specific categories. In particular, the Tables sub-category operates at a negative profit, which signals a structural issue that could require pricing adjustments, cost optimization, or portfolio review

## Strategic Reconnendations
- Review pricing and discount policies for Tables (sub-category)
- Analyse cost structure of low-margin high-revenue products.
- Prioritize Technology category in growth strategies.
- Monitor Consumer segment profitability beyond volume metrics.
